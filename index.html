Step 1 of 5

The task SACE actually sets

Assessment Type 3 — Issues Study asks you to investigate one philosophical issue from any of the key areas (Ethics, Epistemology, Metaphysics, or any of their sub-areas — Political Philosophy, Philosophy of Religion, Philosophy of Mind, Aesthetics). You negotiate the question with your teacher.

Five things you must address

  1. Why is this a philosophical issue?
  2. What positions do various philosophers hold on it?
  3. What are their reasons for holding those positions?
  4. What objections or counter-examples are relevant to those positions?
  5. What is your own position, and how do you defend it?

Your response can be written (up to 2,000 words), oral (up to 12 minutes), or multimodal (an equivalent combination). Either way, you're graded on seven criteria: KU1-2, RA1-3, CA1, and C1-2.

What separates an A from a C+

The single biggest pattern across SACE-graded exemplars: the A response weaves objections into each position, while the C+ response describes one view at a time and never lets them fight.

  • The A integrates. Every philosophical position is presented with both its strongest argument and its strongest objection embedded together. Counter-arguments don't sit in a separate section.
  • The A names. At least four or five philosophers, by name, with their actual reasoning — not "some thinkers argue".
  • The A defends a nuanced own position. Not "I agree with X" but "I agree with X under condition Y, here's why Z".
  • The A uses primary sources. Quotes from the philosophers themselves, not just summaries.
  • The C+ describes. "X believes A. Y believes B. I think B." No critical pressure, no engagement, no conditional commitment.

The seven criteria

  • KU1 — knowledge and understanding of the philosophical issue
  • KU2 — knowledge of various philosophers' positions and their reasoning
  • RA1 — recognition of the philosophical nature of the issue
  • RA2 — use of logic and evidence in reasoning
  • RA3 — formulation and defence of your own position
  • CA1 — critical analysis of strengths, weaknesses, objections
  • C1, C2 — communication: clarity, precision, references

        • Steel-man the view I find weakest.
        • Where in the cached texts is X discussed? Quote a passage.
        • I want to argue Y. What's the most damaging counter I'd have to address?
